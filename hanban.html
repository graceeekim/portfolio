Hanban visually and phonetically connects Hangul, the Korean alphabet, and English Latin characters to create a bilingual typeface. The name is a play on the typeface’s concept, with Han referring to the han in Hangul, and ban, which means half. The design honours the original geometric and logical approach taken to create Hangul and the design of its characters, so I used phonetics to combine the letters to their closest counterpart, and designed them in line with Hangul’s original geometric design.

When using Hanban, text should be read as normal—but with the inclusion of Hangul, reading Hanban characters means you are reading Hangul as well. However, due to the way sounds and words are built differently in both languages, it isn't a 100% match/translation.
